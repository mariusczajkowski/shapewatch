  • What is Shapewatch?

    Shapewatch is a next generation body monitoring technology that allows users to scan themselves, visualise their body shape and measurements in 3D, and track their progress towards their fitness goals.

    The motivational impact of visualising body shape is far greater than viewing numbers alone. Shapewatch combines both and provides a convenient, easy-to-use and time-efficient digital solution that supports users, whatever their fitness goal.

    It consists of two components, the Shapewatch kiosk (hardware) and the Shapewatch app on the user’s mobile device.

How does it work?

Please note, an instruction video plays in a loop on the Shapewatch kiosk to remind unfamiliar users of the scan protocol.

If for any reason the scanning process stalls part way through, and users are unable to complete their scans, they can ask someone else to activate the de-bug feature, by hitting the small [?] icon on the Shapewatch kiosk. The kiosk will then record the settings within its IT configuration, which will then be uploaded to our servers in order to analyse the root cause of the service disruption.

Initiate scan

In order to initiate a scan the user simply hits the [+] symbol on their Shapewatch app which generates a unique QR code on the users Shapewatch app.

The user then presents this QR code to the QR reader on the Shapewatch kiosk, and awaits further instruction from the kiosk.

Scan

1.The kiosk will firstly ask the user to step on the weighing scales to capture their current weight, which the scales register via Bluetooth on the users Shapewatch app.

2.The user is then asked to adopt the first scanning pose (front facing) on the mat, highlighted through the kiosk instructions, standing behind the line on the kiosk, and turning the red icons to green in order for the scan to initiate.

3.The kiosk provides an audible instruction to breathe out and relax. Users should expel as much air from their lungs as possible as this impacts their body measurements.

4.The user is then guided into the second scan position (side scan), again highlighted through the kiosk instructions, by turning the red icons to green in order for the scan to initiate.

5.The two scans are then processed in the cloud, and moments later the outcomes can be reviewed on the users Shapewatch app.

Review

The user’s smartphone app stores the results from all previous scans so users can quickly and easily visualise the changes experienced between scans in 360 degrees, simply by scrolling across the timeline on their Shapewatch app.

Users can highlight a variety of body parts on the Shapewatch app (by hitting the magnifying glass icon) in order to focus their review on these specific regions, which again can be viewed historically using the timeline, and again can be viewed in 360 degrees.

Finally their measurements can be reviewed by hitting the graph icon.

What does it all mean?

Shapewatch allows users to visualise their shape change from one scan to the next, and over time this flow of visuals and metrics builds up to form a more comprehensive view of a user’s shape change.

In addition, the Shapewatch app captures some simple yet important body metrics, including weight (kg), body fat (%), lean body mass (%), WHR (waist to hip ratio), chest circumference (cm), waist circumference (cm), and hip circumference (cm). Note – these metrics will be supplemented very soon by an option to display in Imperial measurements rather than Metric (current default).

Please note the key role that Waist to Hip Ratio plays as an indicator of health. Those individuals that carry higher levels of fat around their abdomen (visceral fat) have a greater chance of developing serious health conditions such as heart disease and diabetes than those that carry weight around their hips.

Please note

Please also note the critical difference in density of muscle and fat. Muscle is approximately 18% more dense than fat, with 1kg of muscle occupying less space (volume) than 1kg of fat. This is why when people lose body fat, their weight often remains unchanged as the ‘larger’ (less dense) fat is shrinking off their body and being replaced by ‘leaner’ (more dense) muscle tissue.

Their total body volume and body measurements, such as waist circumference are reducing in tandem with the reduction in their body fat percentage, all of which are more important measures of improving health than weight alone. This is a vital component in understanding body transformation, with appropriate messaging to members around this being key to their ongoing adherence.

What’s next?

Users will be intrigued by what the Shapewatch kiosk does, so there will be a requirement for club teams to provide some basic guidance until users understand the scanning protocol.

It can be a humbling and at times overwhelming experience to see your body shape highlighted in 360 degrees, so throughout the process please talk with your members about this, particularly those that may have expressed concern over their appearance. Be aware that many people who are overweight don’t see themselves as such, so visualising their shape can be a vivid reality check for them.

Talk your members through their Shapewatch experience and the visuals / metrics involved, providing support where required. Be mindful that the emotional response provoked by Shapewatch can become a very powerful way to promote personal training packages, small group training sessions, or even body transformation programmes.

Software Updates

As Shapewatch is still in the final stages of development, software updates on both the kiosk and user app will continue to progress.

Some of these will be as a result of additional features the Shapewatch team are adding in to enhance the experience, while others may be added as a result of user feedback from within your club. These updates should not provide any interruption in service as they will be internally tested before being released to the trial.

The Shapewatch team will provide you with warnings of these updates and may require some basic assistance in administering these changes in your club. Should this be the case you will be notified.

Feedback

A member survey has been automated to follow a Shapewatch user’s first scanning process which will ask them about their user experience, and should take them no longer than a minute to complete. This user feedback is critical for the Shapewatch team to fine-tune the member experience, so please where possible ask your members to complete the survey.

Additionally, ongoing member feedback is particularly welcomed to inform the Shapewatch team about ongoing adoption, and for suggestions for future roadmap innovations. This feedback could be captured within the club and emailed to feedback@shapewatch.com

Troubleshooting

While Shapewatch has been thoroughly tested by our internal teams, there may still be some unforeseen glitches in the programming that may result in an interruption of service. Should this happen, club teams should make contact as follows  (in order of priority):

Basic IT training will be provided at installation to assist the club teams should a fault occur.

The most common fault scenarios are listed below:

Kiosk goes off
Check power cable / on-off switch

“Connect Structure Sensor”
Close Shapewatch app on kiosk then re-start app

“Please enter SSID” (Grey box)
Check club’s WIFI

Scan not completing
Record feedback footage using ? icon on kiosk
